Frequently Asked Questions

What are preselling condos?

Preselling condominiums are residential properties that developers offer for sale while the project is still in the building stage. This gives prospective purchasers the opportunity to reserve a unit ahead of schedule and benefit from early purchase incentives.

Is it true that a condominium's lifespan is set at 50 years?

No, it's important to remember that you are joining a corporation when you buy a condominium unit. For this reason, property management or building administrators are employed in every condominium building. The condominium doesn't last forever, but the corporation in charge of it does. Normally, corporations last for 50 years, although they can be extended for another period of time.

Can foreigners buy a condominium unit?

In fact, foreigners are allowed to own all of the condominium condominiums in the Philippines, provided that Filipino nationals own at least 60% of the building overall. This clause permits a harmonious balance between domestic and foreign investments by guaranteeing adherence to the laws governing foreign ownership in the real estate industry.

Are there hidden charges in purchasing a condominium unit?

At Megaworld, the properties we offer are VAT-inclusive, meaning that the value-added tax is already incorporated into the purchase price. However, it is important to note that upon property turnover, there are additional government taxes and move-in fees to be settled. These fees encompass the following components:


  • 1. Documentary Stamp Tax
  • 2. Transfer Tax
  • 3. Registration fee
  • 4. Processing fee
  • 5. Real Estate Tax
  • 6. Meralco Fee
  • 7. Move-in fee

Please be informed that these charges are separate from the VAT and are required to be settled in order to complete the necessary legal and administrative processes associated with property acquisition.
