Future developments in Fort
Bonifacio, Taguig City

Metro Manila Subway Project

The Philippines' first-ever underground mass transport system. The Metro Manila Subway is a 36-kilometer rail line that is an infrastructure project aimed at reducing travel time between Quezon City and the Ninoy Aquino International Airport to just 30 minutes with 17 stations across Metro Manila's municipalities.

According to the Department of Transportation, the project's overall completion is at 25.09% and is expected to be fully operational by the third quarter of 2027.

BGC-Ortigas Link Bridge Or Kalayaan Bridge

Completed on June 12, 2021, the Kalayaan bridge (formerly BGC-Ortigas Bridge) is a four-lane, two-way bridge that connects Lawton Avenue and Kapitolyo across the Pasig River.

The bridge aims to reduce the traffic congestion of road networks such as C-5 and EDSA, while reducing the travel time from Fort Bonifacio to Ortigas to just 12 minutes.

Expansion Of Lawton Avenue

Complementing the Kalayaan Bridge is the expansion of Lawton Avenue from four lanes to six lanes. A road network that covers from 5th avenue to Bayani Road, the widening project has eased the traffic situations along EDSA, South Superhighway, and C-5 Road, while reducing the travel time from Bonifacio Global City to Nino Aquino International Airport to only 15 minutes.

New Senate Of The Philippines

Situated in a 20,000-sqm or 2-hectare parcel of land owned by the Bases Conversion and Development Authority (BCDA) located at the former Navy Village, Fort Bonifacio.

Skytrain By Infracorp

INFRACORP, a new company created by conglomerate Alliance Global Group Inc., submitted the unsolicited proposal "Skytrain". The project, to benefit around 60,000 to 100,000 passengers daily, will reduce travel time from Uptown BGC to MRT Guadalupe to only five minutes.

Future developments in Bulacan

New Manila International Airport

New Manila International Airport will be the biggest and most sophisticated airport outside of Metro Manila and It is currently being built on a 2,565-ha site in Bulacan. It is exactly 35km away from Metro Manila and 20-minutes away from Megaworld's newest township in Marilao/Bocaue, Bulacan which is Northwin Global Clty.

This $15-billion project of San Miguel Corporation will have four runways, eight taxiways, and three terminals. The airport aims to handle 100 million passengers per year once fully completed.

This airport will be connected to a future 8km airport toll road that can be accessed through North Luzon Expressway and the 18km Skyway Stage 3 that leads to the South Luzon Expressway.

North to South Commuter Railway (Clark-Malolos-Tutuban-Calamba)

Phase 1 of the NSCR is a 38-km railway system that will connect Malolos, Bulacan and Tutuban, Manila while the 52-km long phase 2 will connect Malolos and Clark, Pampanga.

In the southern part of Luzon, a 56-km long railway system will connect Solis, Manila and Calamba, Laguna which will provide a more sustainable and efficient transportation system in the surrounding provinces/cities of Metro Manila.

MRT 7

The Metro Rail Transit Line 7 (MRT 7) project is a 23-kilometer elevated railway line with 14 stations from San Jose Del Monte, Bulacan to MRT 3 North Avenue in Quezon City which will make daily commutes from Quezon City to San Jose Del Monte, Bulacan faster and easier.

About the Company

Megaworld Corporation

Megaworld Corporation, established by Dr. Andrew Tan on August 24, 1989, under Philippine law, is a prominent figure in the real estate sector, known for its development, leasing, and marketing prowess. Initially renowned for its construction of high-end residential condominiums and commercial properties in strategic urban locales across Metro Manila, Megaworld pivoted towards mixed-use community development in 1996. This transition, exemplified by projects like Eastwood City, catered to the burgeoning demand for integrated living, working, and leisure spaces, particularly targeting the middle-income demographic. Alongside its primary focus on real estate development, Megaworld also engages in ancillary activities such as project design, construction supervision, and property management, ensuring comprehensive service delivery.

A significant milestone in its corporate evolution occurred on August 19, 1999, when Megaworld rebranded to reflect its transformation into a holding company, while maintaining its commitment to real estate excellence. Over the past 33 years, Megaworld has solidified its position as the foremost developer of residential and office spaces in the Philippines, outpacing competitors and adapting to changing market dynamics. Dr. Andrew L. Tan, the visionary founder and Chairman, pioneered the Live-Work-Play-Learn township concept, integrating residential buildings, office towers, commercial hubs, and luxury accommodations within vibrant communities. This innovative approach not only sets Megaworld apart but also underscores its dedication to creating holistic urban environments that cater to diverse lifestyle needs.
